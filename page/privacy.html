Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 25, 2026
Website: https://aw-junaid.github.io/
Contact: awjunaid@proton.me

1. Introduction

Welcome to AW Junaid's Cybersecurity Blog ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our blog, interact with our content, or communicate with us.

As a cybersecurity-focused publication, we practice what we preach: minimal data collection, strong encryption, transparency, and user control are foundational to our operations.

By accessing or using our blog, you consent to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our site.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Voluntarily

  • Contact Information: When you email us at awjunaid@proton.me, we collect your email address, name (if provided), and message content.
  • Comments/Feedback: If our platform supports comments, we may collect your name, email, and comment content. Note: We do not require accounts for commenting to minimize data exposure.
  • Newsletter Subscriptions: If offered, we collect only your email address. Subscription is opt-in, and you may unsubscribe at any time.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Log Data: Our hosting provider (GitHub Pages) may collect standard server logs including:
    • IP address (anonymized where possible)
    • Browser type and version
    • Operating system
    • Referring URL
    • Pages visited and time spent
    • Date/time of access
  • Cookies & Similar Technologies:
    • Essential Cookies: Required for site functionality (e.g., session management).
    • Analytics Cookies: We do not use third-party analytics (e.g., Google Analytics) by default to protect visitor privacy. If enabled in the future, it will be opt-in only with anonymized IP tracking.
    • Preference Cookies: Store your display preferences (e.g., dark mode) locally in your browser.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We do not purchase or aggregate personal data from third-party data brokers. If you interact with us via social media or linked resources, those platforms' privacy policies apply.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information solely for:

  • Responding to your inquiries and providing support
  • Improving blog content, security, and user experience
  • Sending critical security updates or policy changes (if subscribed)
  • Protecting against fraud, abuse, or unauthorized access
  • Complying with legal obligations

We do not:

  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal data
  • Use your data for targeted advertising
  • Profile users for behavioral marketing

4. Data Security Practices (Cybersecurity-First Approach)

Given our focus on cybersecurity, we implement rigorous safeguards:

✅ Encryption: All data in transit is protected via HTTPS/TLS 1.3. Sensitive communications via ProtonMail benefit from end-to-end encryption.

✅ Minimal Retention:

  • Email inquiries: Retained only as long as necessary to resolve your request (typically ≤90 days), then securely deleted.
  • Logs: Aggregated/anonymized logs retained ≤30 days for security monitoring, then purged.

✅ Access Controls: Strict role-based access; only authorized personnel can access personal data.

✅ Vulnerability Management: Regular security audits, dependency scanning, and prompt patching of our static site infrastructure.

✅ No Database Storage: As a static GitHub Pages site, we store no user databases. Personal data exists only in encrypted email inboxes or transient logs.

✅ Incident Response: In the unlikely event of a breach affecting your data, we will notify affected users and relevant authorities within 72 hours, per GDPR standards.

5. Third-Party Services & Links

5.1 Integrated Services

  • GitHub Pages: Our hosting provider. Review GitHub's Privacy Policy.
  • ProtonMail: Our email service. Review Proton's Privacy Policy.
  • External Links: Our blog may link to third-party resources (tools, research, news). We are not responsible for their privacy practices. Always review their policies before sharing data.

5.2 Embedded Content

Articles may embed content from YouTube, GitHub Gists, or code sandbox tools. These embeds may set cookies or collect data per their own policies. We minimize embedded trackers and disclose them where used.

6. Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights:

🔹 Access & Portability: Request a copy of your personal data we hold.
🔹 Correction: Update inaccurate or incomplete information.
🔹 Deletion: Request erasure of your data ("right to be forgotten").
🔹 Restriction/Objection: Limit or object to certain processing activities.
🔹 Withdraw Consent: Opt out of non-essential communications anytime.
🔹 Cookie Control: Manage cookie preferences via your browser settings.

To exercise these rights, contact us at awjunaid@proton.me with "Privacy Request" in the subject line. We respond within 30 days.

Note: As a small static blog, our data holdings are minimal. Most "requests" can be fulfilled immediately.

7. Children's Privacy

Our content is intended for general audiences, including students and professionals in cybersecurity. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16 (or 13 in the U.S.). If we learn we have inadvertently collected such data, we will delete it promptly. Parents/guardians may contact us to request removal.

8. International Data Transfers

Our services are hosted globally via GitHub's infrastructure. Data may be processed in jurisdictions outside your own. We ensure transfers comply with applicable frameworks (e.g., GDPR Chapter V), relying on adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses, or user consent as required.

  • Material changes will be posted prominently on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.
  • Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
  • For significant changes, we may notify subscribers via email.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
